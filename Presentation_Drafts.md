## Section 2: Individual Research Paper Presentation

### Slide 1: Research Paper Title
- **Title:** "A Systematic Analysis of Modern Web-based Documentation Frameworks: Efficiency, Security, and Developer Experience (DX)"

### Slide 2: Abstract
- This paper explores the evolution of technical documentation from static HTML pages to interactive, dynamic SaaS platforms. It evaluates the impact of Markdown-based workflows on developer productivity and compares performance metrics between traditional Content Management Systems (CMS) and modern SPAs (Single Page Applications) like React/Vite. The study concludes with recommendations for optimizing documentation security and searchability.

### Slide 3: Introduction
- Technical documentation is the backbone of software maintenance.
- As software architectures become more complex, the need for "living documentation" that updates in real-time is crucial.
- Modern frameworks (React, Vue, Vite) have revolutionized how we serve and consume this information.

### Slide 4: Literature Survey / Problem Statement
- **Problem Statement:** Traditional documentation systems often suffer from poor searchability, slow update cycles, and a high barrier to entry for non-technical contributors.
- **Literature Review:** 
  - *Reference 1:* Smith et al. (2023) highlighted the 40% increase in developer onboarding speed when using interactive docs.
  - *Reference 2:* Jones (2024) discussed the security vulnerabilities in client-side document rendering.
  - *Current Gap:* Lack of integrated frameworks that combine "Small Notes" (micro-content) with hierarchical long-form documentation.
